Custom Autonomous Quadcopter

A high-performance drone built from the ground up, featuring custom PID tuning and waypoint navigation.

Project Overview

This project began as an exploration into flight dynamics and control theory. Unlike off-the-shelf commercial drones, building a quadcopter from individual components requires a deep understanding of power distribution, signal processing, and mechanical balance.

The objective was to create a stable, autonomous flight platform capable of executing GPS-guided missions while maintaining a high thrust-to-weight ratio for agility.

Technical Implementation

Power Distribution: I designed a custom wiring harness to connect the 4S LiPo battery to the Electronic Speed Controllers (ESCs). This required careful consideration of current draw (peaking at 80A) to prevent voltage sag during rapid maneuvers.

Flight Control Logic: The core of the system is an STM32-based flight controller running ArduPilot. I spent significant time tuning the PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) loops. Initially, the drone suffered from oscillations in the roll axis, which I resolved by filtering sensor noise from the IMU and adjusting the 'D' gain.

Fabrication: The frame was assembled using carbon fiber arms for rigidity and 3D-printed TPU mounts for the camera and antenna to dampen vibrations.

Technical Specs

Microcontroller STM32 F405
Motors 2300KV Brushless
Battery System 4S LiPo (14.8V)
Communication 2.4GHz RC / 915MHz Telemetry
Software Betaflight / QGroundControl
